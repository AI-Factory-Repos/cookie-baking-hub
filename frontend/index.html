📖 Baking Terminology ▼
Cream
Beat butter and sugar together vigorously until the mixture is pale, light, and fluffy (about 3–5 min with a mixer). This incorporates air for tender cookies.
Fold
Gently combine ingredients using a spatula in a sweeping under-and-over motion. Used to avoid deflating whipped eggs or over-developing gluten.
Room temperature
Butter and eggs at ~68–72°F (20–22°C). Cold butter won't cream properly; cold eggs can cause the batter to curdle. Leave them out 30–60 min before baking.
Chill the dough
Refrigerate cookie dough (usually 30 min to overnight) so cookies spread less and develop more complex flavour.
Soft peaks / stiff peaks
Stages of whipped cream or egg whites. Soft peaks droop when the beater is lifted; stiff peaks stand straight up.
Scant
Slightly less than the stated amount — e.g. "a scant teaspoon" means just under 1 tsp.
Heaped / heaping
More than the stated amount, mounded above the rim of the spoon.
Zest
The colourful outer rind of citrus fruit, grated finely. Contains essential oils that add bright flavour. Avoid the white pith — it's bitter.
Bain-marie / double boiler
A bowl set over a pot of barely simmering water to gently melt chocolate or heat eggs without scorching.
Bench rest
Leaving dough to rest on the counter for a few minutes so the gluten relaxes and it becomes easier to roll or shape.

🔍 Oven Tips for Beginners

  • Preheat fully: Always let your oven reach the target temperature before baking — at least 10–15 minutes.
  • Oven thermometer: Most home ovens run 25–50°F off. A $10 oven thermometer is one of the best baking investments you can make.
  • Fan / convection: Reduce the recipe temperature by 25°F (15°C) when using a fan-assisted / convection setting.
  • Rack position: Bake cookies in the centre of the oven for the most even heat. Rotate the tray halfway through for best results.
  • Dark vs light pans: Dark pans absorb more heat and can over-brown the bottoms. Prefer light or medium-coloured pans, or reduce temp by 25°F.
👀 Visual Cues for Doneness ▼
😐

Underdone

  • Very shiny, wet-looking surface
  • Edges have not set; dough still spreads when touched
  • Pale all over with no colour on edges
  • Sinks in completely when pressed

➕ Add 2–3 more minutes

😍

Perfect

  • Edges are set and lightly golden
  • Centre looks slightly underdone / glossy — this is correct!
  • Cookie springs back slowly when gently pressed
  • Smells nutty and buttery

✅ Remove from oven now — it will firm up as it cools

😬

Overdone

  • Deep brown or dark edges
  • Very firm / crunchy when still hot
  • Cracked surface with a dry appearance
  • Smells slightly burnt

⚠️ Next batch: reduce time by 2 min or lower temp 25°F

💡 Carry-over cooking: Cookies continue cooking on the hot tray for 2–3 minutes after leaving the oven. Always let them cool on the tray before moving!
